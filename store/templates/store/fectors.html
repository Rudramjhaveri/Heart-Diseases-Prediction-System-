Heart Disease Prediction Factors

Understanding the key indicators of heart health

1. Age

Risk increases with age as blood vessels become less flexible and heart function changes. Regular check-ups become crucial for monitoring cardiovascular health in older adults.

2. Gender

Men face higher risks earlier in life. Women's risk increases after menopause due to reduced estrogen protection. Both genders need to maintain heart-healthy lifestyles.

3. Chest Pain Types

Different types of chest pain can indicate various heart conditions, ranging from typical angina to non-cardiac pain. Understanding the type helps in diagnosis.

4. Resting Blood Pressure

High blood pressure strains the heart and blood vessels. Normal range is typically below 120/80 mmHg. Regular monitoring is essential.

5. Cholesterol

High cholesterol leads to arterial plaque buildup. Managing both LDL (bad) and HDL (good) cholesterol is key to heart health.

6. Fasting Blood Sugar

Elevated levels (>120 mg/dl) suggest diabetes risk. Diabetes significantly increases the likelihood of heart disease development.

7. Resting ECG Results

ECG readings show heart rhythm and potential abnormalities. Regular ECG tests help detect early signs of heart problems.

8. Maximum Heart Rate

Lower maximum heart rate during exercise may indicate cardiovascular issues. Age-related changes affect normal ranges.

9. Exercise-Induced Angina

Chest pain during physical activity suggests reduced blood flow to the heart. A key indicator of coronary artery disease.

10. ST Depression

ECG changes during exercise indicate potential heart stress. The degree of depression correlates with disease severity.

11. ST Segment Slope

The slope pattern during stress tests helps evaluate heart condition. Different patterns indicate varying levels of risk.

12. Major Vessels

Number of blocked major blood vessels affects heart disease severity. More blockages indicate higher risk levels.
